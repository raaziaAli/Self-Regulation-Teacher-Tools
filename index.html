Growing Tomorrow's Citizens in Today's Classrooms

Critical Competencies and Classroom Assessment Tools

This page highlights seven critical competencies for future-ready learners and provides three practical classroom tools you can use to gather evidence of students' growth in these areas.

Seven Critical Competencies

The framework below summarizes seven key competencies that support students in becoming thoughtful, responsible, and engaged citizens. You can adjust the titles and descriptions to match the exact language used in the book.

  • 1.
    Self-Awareness and Identity
    Understanding personal strengths, values, and areas for growth; recognizing how one’s background and experiences shape perspectives.
  • 2.
    Empathy and Perspective-Taking
    Listening to and considering the feelings, needs, and points of view of others; learning to see issues from multiple angles.
  • 3.
    Critical and Creative Thinking
    Asking meaningful questions, analyzing information, and generating imaginative solutions to complex problems.
  • 4.
    Collaboration and Community
    Working productively with others, contributing to group goals, and building a sense of shared responsibility in the classroom.
  • 5.
    Communication and Dialogue
    Expressing ideas clearly, asking clarifying questions, and engaging in respectful discussion and disagreement.
  • 6.
    Ethical Decision-Making
    Considering the impact of choices on self, others, and the wider community; acting with integrity and fairness.
  • 7.
    Agency and Civic Engagement
    Seeing oneself as capable of taking informed action, contributing to change, and participating in local and global communities.

How to Use This Page

Use the tools below as quick, low-prep ways to gather evidence of how students are developing these competencies in your classroom. Each activity can be used:

  • as a warm-up or reflection task,
  • as part of advisory or homeroom, or
  • as formative assessment alongside academic units.

You might invite students to revisit these tools over time and discuss how their responses change as they grow as learners and citizens.

Classroom Assessment Strategies

These three interactive tools provide different windows into students' dispositions, habits, and hopes. Each connects naturally to multiple competencies in the framework above.

DNA Inventory

A self-reflection inventory that invites students to identify their "learning DNA": personal strengths, working styles, and core values as learners and classmates.

Self-awareness Identity Student voice

Use at the start of the year or term to get to know students and to make their strengths and needs visible. Revisit later to discuss growth and shifting identities.

Open DNA Inventory

Effort Tracker

A simple tool for students to monitor their effort, focus, and use of strategies over time, rather than only tracking grades or outcomes.

Agency Metacognition Growth mindset

Use weekly or after key tasks. Encourage students to notice patterns, set small goals, and connect effort to progress in both academic work and citizenship competencies.

Open Effort Tracker

Hope Survey

A short survey that surfaces students' sense of hope, belonging, and purpose in school, and how they see their future selves as learners and citizens.

Belonging Well-being Future orientation

Use at key points in the year to open conversation about classroom climate, support systems, and how school experiences connect to students' goals and dreams.

Open Hope Survey
